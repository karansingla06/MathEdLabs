Experiential Math Learning

Experiential learning is based on the active involvement of each learner that enhances and amplifies their learning. Manipulatives and other STEM resources are useful in helping children move from abstract to concrete level. A teacher must choose these activities carefully to make them understand the concept. When a student is involved in active and useful experiences they feel competent & confident to solve real life problems. Math Ed Labs provide an opportunity to experience mathematics learning through the hands on mode. Putting knowledge to practical uses presents an ideal avenue for the application of mathematics skills. The purpose for learning is evident with a coherent, integrated curriculum, freeing students to reason about complex problems, analyze multiple solutions, and communicate ideas and results. They develop habits of mind along with the necessary mathematics skills.

Services

The team aims to serve schools in terms of its hands on products for learners, need-based resources mathematics lab at schools, workshops for students and teachers, also specific group based projects for assessment which lets them apply their learning. From hands on learning tools to math lab resources, activity booklets, worksheets, modules for assessment to provide best possible resources to learner. We conduct workshops for students as well as Teachers. The main focus always revolves around making mathematics more exploratory, experience based, Do it yourself and Hands on.

Workshops

The team at Math Ed Labs has developed workshops based on many math concepts with DIY activities. Workshops are based on different themes which help learners to understand and enjoy the construction of knowledge through experiments and discussions. Also, the workshops planned are in synchronization with the curriculum which can be easily mapped with the content in the textbooks.

Concept Based Kits

Hands on learning mathematical tools for school learner aged 5-17 years which gives them an experience to visualize mathematics & experience it on their own. The tools are designed on a concept rather than the traditional grade based curriculum. Math Lab Resources

Mathematics laboratory is a place rich in manipulation materials to which students have ready access to handle them, perform mathematical experiments, play mathematical games, solve mathematical puzzles and become involved in other activities. Math Ed labs provide best possible resources for schools & educational organization’s math labs. Learners can use mathematical DIY manipulatives, verify their concepts, and learn new math concepts with different resources.

Project Based Learning

We build project for learners to explore mathematics on their own in a playful way with free-play as well as with directed instructions to accelerate their thinking process. Learners are exposed to the real world math phenomenon. They are given tasks which include situations from their daily life. This helps in building concepts around the situation. These exercises convey the value of learning a lesson and displays real life application to the students.

After School Programme

We organize full time and part time program for learners after their school. This helps them to innovate and learn new mathematical skills, explore mathematics in nature and apply their learning to real life mathematical problems.

What We Do

Math Ed Labs is working towards the shift in pedagogy of mathematics teaching-learning. To set up a platform where the participants i.e. the teachers and the students, will get that extra space where they have the chance to discover their abilities and improvise on their skills, moreover, they have freedom to experiment with their methods of teaching and learning.

We Aim to develop a culture of math teaching & learning through methods (Do it yourself, hands on) which are capable of constructing knowledge as well as allow teachers to implement the same innovative methodology while interacting with students

WHY

Math is considered as a rote learning exercise and memory based science, which is taught with the help of a calculative approach. Students are not able to visualize and experience math concepts. This makes many students scared of mathematics and leaves only a handful of students willing to study math. The phobia of mathematics in students is because of their inability to conceptualise and implement the use of any concept in their everyday life.


The solution to the problem lies in the fundamentals of the classroom practices. The need to 'make the student understand' should be the ultimate goal for a teacher.

How We Do

Hands On Learning

The use of manipulatives in teaching mathematics has a long tradition and solid research history. Manipulatives not only allow students to construct their own cognitive models for abstract mathematical ideas and processes. In addition to the ability of manipulatives to aid directly in the cognitive process, manipulatives have the additional advantage of engaging students and increasing both interest in and enjoyment of mathematics.

Making Mathematics fun

The seemingly simple question of solving math has plagued countless generations of parents and children who have gone through our education system. Solving math questions can be difficult, but what makes it worse is if a child has no interest in the subject. To encourage a child to learn math, we must help him or her to develop interest.

Innovative tools

The education system needs to move away from the conventional pedagogy of teaching mathematics especially at school level and to include methods of facilitating learning. Innovative and effective practices make the learning process lively and let the participants share a new vision, which ultimately leads to the higher aims of education.

Three-tier Scheme

Learning should happen gradually and steadily. To retain a memory, the experience should be rich as well as involve the learner in their own concept development process. The ideation of a concept is a stage where the environment is playful and the expected responses are open ended. The next stage is to experiment with the responses and direct thoughts to a direction through reasoning and assessment. Later, the opinion made is shaped into a concept.
